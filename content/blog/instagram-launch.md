## A New Kind of Gathering

For months now, this table has been a place of words. Recipes, stories, histories — the long, slow conversations that unfold through sentences and paragraphs. It has been, in its own way, a kind of feast. But a table is not a table if no one new can pull up a chair.

So today, we're happy to share this: La Nostra Tavola is coming to Instagram.

We'll be at **@lanostratavola** — and yes, the name means exactly what you think it means. *Our table.* We're setting it up in a new corner of the world, and we hope you'll join us there.

<div class="pull-quote">
  "A table without guests is just a piece of furniture. Instagram, for all its noise, is still a place where people gather. We want to be part of the gathering."
</div>

## What You'll Find There

The Instagram table will be different from this one, but related — like the difference between a full Sunday lunch and an afternoon coffee with a friend. It will be lighter. More immediate. A place for the things that happen between the long essays.

You can expect:

**Behind the table.** The moments that don't make it into the stories — the steam rising from a pot of *cicoria* on a cold morning, the way light falls across a farmer's market in early spring, the hands of the woman making orecchiette in Bari Vecchia.

**Seasonal snapshots.** What we're cooking, what we're reading, what's in season right now. A photo of *carciofi violetti* at the market. A short video of pasta being folded. A quick note about the wine we opened last night.

**Stories in miniature.** Some stories want to be told in a single image. The color of olive oil in a glass. The pattern of salt on a freshly baked loaf. The way a fisherman's hands tell their own story without a single word.

**Your table, too.** We want to see where you eat, what you cook, who you share your meals with. Use **#lanostratavola** or tag us. Send us your questions about that tricky artichoke prep. Show us the wine you found. The table grows wider the more people sit at it.

## What Won't Change

This is not a pivot. The long-form stories — the deep dives into regional traditions, the recipes with their full context, the wine pairings and the histories — those will continue here, on this site, at the pace and length they deserve. Instagram is not a replacement. It is an invitation.

If you love the long reads, they will still be here. If you want something shorter and more immediate, the Instagram table will be waiting.

## The Word We Keep Coming Back To

*Tavola.* Table. But also: board. Slab. The surface on which things happen.

In Italian, when you say *mettere in tavola*, you mean "to put food on the table." But the phrase carries a deeper sense — the act of making something available, of offering, of hosting. *Mettere in tavola* is not just about serving dinner. It is about creating the conditions for a meal to become a memory.

That's what we want to do here, there, everywhere. Create the conditions.

So come find us. Pull up a chair. The table is ready.
